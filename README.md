# 📱 Does Scrolling Kill Your GPA?

### I ran the numbers on 30 students — and the statistics are shocking.

---

We all know someone who's constantly on Instagram or TikTok between classes.  
But **what does the data actually say?**

I surveyed **30 university students** on their daily social media usage and CGPA — then applied real statistical methods to find the truth.

---

## 🔢 What the Numbers Revealed

📊 **Average screen time → 4.27 hours/day**
That's more than half a typical workday — just on social media.

📉 **Correlation between screen time & CGPA → r = −0.9918**
Nearly perfect negative relationship. More scrolling = lower grades. Every. Single. Time.

🔴 **P(Low CGPA | High Usage) = 71.4%**
If a student uses social media more than 4 hrs/day, there's a **71.4% chance** their CGPA is below 3.0.

🔄 **Bayes' Rule result:**
Flip the question — *"A student has a low CGPA. What's the probability they're a heavy social media user?"*
→ **83.3%**

That's not a coincidence. That's mathematics.
